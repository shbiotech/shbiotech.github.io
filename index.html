About

TSA allows students to explore the realms of technology, engineering, science, and mathematics. TSA at Biotech allows students to explore projects and events outside their mainstream science courses. With events requiring skills in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics, TSA allows students to develop these skills while giving them leadership, management,and academic opportunities. Navigate through this site to find out more about TSA competitions and events.

Science Competitions


Biotechnology Design

A combination of science and engineering, students must select a current biotechnology problem and develop a solution, showing complete understanding of the topic through detailed research, a display, and a multimedia presentation.

Forensic Science

The Forensic Science competition takes place in 2 parts. First, participants must take a written test on forensic science theory. Those who qualify as semifinalists will then examine a mock crime scene and demonstrate their knowledge, using proper techniques to collect evidence and write a detailed analysis on their findings.

Scientific Visualization (SciVis)

Participants will use computer graphics tools to communicate, analyze, and illustrate a STEM concept.
